# Spectral reflectance / Reflectancia espectral

> **level 0** · *data* · sources: th_remote_sensing

**What it is / Qué es.**

- **EN.** The fraction of incident [[electromagnetic-radiation|electromagnetic radiation]] reflected by a surface as a [[function|function]] of [[wavelength|wavelength]], used to distinguish [[land-cover|land cover]] types via their spectral signatures.
- **ES.** Fracción de la [[electromagnetic-radiation|radiación electromagnética]] incidente reflejada por una superficie en [[function|función]] de la [[wavelength|longitud de onda]], utilizada para distinguir tipos de [[land-cover|cobertura del suelo]] mediante sus firmas espectrales.

## This unlocks / Esto habilita
- [[electromagnetic-spectrum|Electromagnetic spectrum]] — It describes how surface materials reflect different portions of the electromagnetic spectrum.
- [[reflectance|Reflectance]] — Reflectance is the ratio of reflected to incident radiation, often derived from spectral reflectance.
- [[radiometric-corrections|Radiometric corrections]] — These corrections ensure spectral reflectance values are accurate and comparable across time and sensors.
- [[satellite-image-classification|Satellite image classification]] — Spectral reflectance patterns from imagery are the basis for classifying satellite images.
- [[chlorophyll-absorption|Chlorophyll absorption]] — Chlorophyll absorption causes specific troughs in the spectral reflectance curve of vegetation.
- [[spectral-signature|Spectral signature]] — A spectral signature is the unique spectral reflectance pattern across wavelengths, distinguishing different surface materials.
