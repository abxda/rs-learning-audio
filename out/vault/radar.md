# Radar / Radar

> **level 4** · *sensor* · sources: th_remote_sensing#introduction-to-remote-sensing-imagery

**What it is / Qué es.**

- **EN.** A [[remote-sensing|remote sensing]] technique that emits [[microwave|microwave]] signals and analyzes the returning echoes to observe the [[earth|Earth]]'s surface, capable of penetrating clouds and operating day or night.
- **ES.** Una técnica de [[remote-sensing|teledetección]] que emite señales de [[microwave|microondas]] y analiza los ecos de retorno para observar la superficie de la [[land|Tierra]], capaz de penetrar nubes y funcionar de día o de noche.

## Understand these first / Entiende esto primero
- [[remote-sensing|Remote sensing]] — *method*
    - **EN.** Radar is a remote sensing technique that uses actively emitted microwave signals instead of passive solar radiation.
    - **ES.** El radar es una técnica de teledetección que utiliza señales de microondas emitidas activamente en lugar de radiación solar pasiva.
- [[sensors|Sensors]] — *sensor*
    - **EN.** Radar sensors on satellites transmit pulses and record echoes to measure surface properties.
    - **ES.** Los sensores de radar en satélites transmiten pulsos y registran ecos para medir propiedades de la superficie.

## This unlocks / Esto habilita
- [[microwave|Microwave]] — Radar operates in the microwave portion of the spectrum, allowing day-and-night and all-weather imaging.
- [[radar-vegetation-index|Radar Vegetation Index]] — Radar vegetation indices use radar backscatter from different polarizations to estimate vegetation structure.
- [[synthetic-aperture-radar|Synthetic Aperture Radar]] — Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) uses the motion of a satellite to simulate a larger antenna aperture.
