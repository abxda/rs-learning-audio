# Satellite / Satélite

> **level 1** · *platform* · sources: th_remote_sensing

**What it is / Qué es.**

- **EN.** An artificial object orbiting [[earth|Earth]] that carries [[sensors|sensors]] to collect [[imagery|imagery]] and measurements for [[earth-observation|Earth observation]] and [[agricultural-monitoring|agricultural monitoring]].
- **ES.** Un objeto artificial que orbita la [[land|Tierra]] y lleva [[sensors|sensores]] para recopilar [[images|imágenes]] y mediciones para la [[earth-observation|observación de la Tierra]] y el [[agricultural-monitoring|monitoreo agrícola]].

## Understand these first / Entiende esto primero
- [[images|Images]] — *data*
    - **EN.** Images are digital representations of Earth captured by satellite sensors.
    - **ES.** Las imágenes son representaciones digitales de la Tierra capturadas por sensores satelitales.

## This unlocks / Esto habilita
- [[sensors|Sensors]] — Sensors are the instruments mounted on satellites that collect imagery and other measurements.
- [[remote-sensing|Remote sensing]] — Remote sensing obtains information about Earth's surface from a distance, often using satellites.
- [[collection|Collection]] — A collection organizes satellite images from specific missions into usable groups.
- [[source|Source]] — A source can be a satellite sensor that acquires images for Earth observation.
- [[large-scale-agricultural-mapping|Large scale agricultural mapping]] — Large-scale agricultural mapping uses satellite imagery to cover wide regions efficiently.
- [[big-data|Big Data]] — Satellite missions generate petabytes of imagery, creating big data challenges for storage and analysis.
- [[satellite-image-time-series|Satellite image time series]] — Satellite image time series are sequences of satellite images of the same location acquired at regular intervals over time.
- [[cover|Cover]] — Satellite sensors measure the reflected electromagnetic radiation determining surface cover.
- [[landsat|Landsat]] — Landsat is a series of Earth-observing satellite missions providing free multispectral imagery for land monitoring.
- [[satellite-sensors|Satellite sensors]] — Satellite sensors are the instruments mounted on satellites that detect electromagnetic radiation to capture images.
- [[planet|Planet]] — Planet is a commercial satellite constellation that provides high-resolution multispectral imagery daily.
- [[food-security|Food security]] — Satellite-based crop monitoring provides timely data to detect food shortages and support food security planning.
- [[temporal-resolution|Temporal resolution]] — Temporal resolution of a satellite mission is determined by its orbit and swath, affecting the revisit frequency.
- [[sentinel-missions|Sentinel Missions]] — The Sentinel missions are a constellation of Earth‑observing satellites developed by the European Space Agency.
- [[geometric-correction|Geometric Correction]] — Geometric correction adjusts the raw coordinates of satellite imagery to align with a reference coordinate system.
- [[gps|GPS]] — GPS uses a constellation of satellites to provide precise geographic coordinates for ground locations.
- [[nasa|NASA]] — NASA is the U.S. space agency that jointly manages Earth-observing satellite missions like Landsat.
- [[synthetic-aperture-radar|Synthetic Aperture Radar]] — A SAR satellite transmits radar pulses and records the echoes to create high-resolution images of the Earth.
- [[satellite-data|Satellite data]] — Satellite data are the measurements collected by sensors aboard satellites orbiting Earth.
- [[satellite-image-collections|Satellite image collections]] — Satellite image collections are organized archives of imagery from satellite missions.
