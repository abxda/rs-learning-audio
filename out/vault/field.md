# Field / Campo

> **level 2** · *phenomenon* · sources: th_parcel_extraction

**What it is / Qué es.**

- **EN.** An [[area|area]] of [[land|land]] cultivated with a single [[crop|crop]] or managed as a unit, typically delineated as a [[polygons|polygon]] in [[geospatial-data|geospatial data]] for [[analysis|analysis]].
- **ES.** Un [[area|área]] de [[land|tierra]] cultivada con un solo [[crop|cultivo]] o gestionada como una unidad, típicamente delimitada como un polígono en [[geospatial-data|datos geoespaciales]] para [[analysis|análisis]].

## Understand these first / Entiende esto primero
- [[land|Land]] — *phenomenon*
    - **EN.** A field is an area of land cultivated with a single crop or managed as a unit.
    - **ES.** Un campo es un área de tierra cultivada con un solo cultivo o manejada como unidad.

## This unlocks / Esto habilita
- [[polygons|Polygons]] — Polygons are vector boundaries delineating individual fields or parcels in geospatial data.
- [[parcels|Parcels]] — Parcels are individual agricultural fields or plots, the fundamental spatial units for crop mapping.
- [[ground-surveys|Ground surveys]] — Ground surveys visit agricultural fields to record crop types, phenology, and other attributes.
- [[individual-agricultural-plot-polygons|Individual agricultural plot polygons]] — Fields are individual cultivated areas represented as polygons in geospatial data.
- [[field-data-collection|Field data collection]] — Field data collection involves gathering ground observations such as crop type and GPS coordinates from agricultural fields.
- [[field-samples|Field samples]] — Field samples are taken from individual fields, representing specific crop types and conditions.
- [[agricultural-plots|Agricultural plots]] — Agricultural plots are individual fields managed as single units for a specific crop.
- [[ground-truth|Ground-truth]] — Field observations of real land cover conditions are directly used to create ground-truth data for training and validation.
- [[in-situ-observations|In-situ observations]] — In-situ observations are collected directly in agricultural fields to record crop type, condition, and other attributes.
