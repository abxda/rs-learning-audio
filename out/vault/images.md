# Images / Imágenes

> **level 0** · *data* · sources: th_remote_sensing

**What it is / Qué es.**

- **EN.** Digital representations of the [[earth|Earth]]'s surface captured by [[satellite-sensors|satellite sensors]], composed of multiple [[spectral-bands|spectral bands]] used for [[analysis|analysis]].
- **ES.** Representaciones digitales de la superficie de la [[land|Tierra]] capturadas por [[satellite-sensors|sensores satelitales]], compuestas por múltiples [[bands|bandas espectrales]] utilizadas para [[analysis|análisis]].

## This unlocks / Esto habilita
- [[satellite|Satellite]] — Images are digital representations of Earth captured by satellite sensors.
- [[pixels|Pixels]] — Pixels are the smallest discrete units in satellite images, each representing a specific land area.
- [[map|Map]] — A map is produced by classifying satellite images into thematic categories.
- [[imagery|Imagery]] — Imagery refers to data collected by satellite sensors, including optical and radar images.
- [[time-series|Time series]] — A satellite image time series combines multiple images of the same location taken at different times.
- [[cloud|Cloud]] — Cloud appears in satellite images as opaque areas that need to be identified and removed.
- [[layers|Layers]] — Images are composed of layers, each representing a specific spectral band or derived data component.
- [[cube|Cube]] — A cube organizes a collection of satellite images into a three-dimensional array indexed by time and band.
- [[remote-image-classification|Remote image classification]] — Remote image classification uses satellite images as the input data to assign class labels.
- [[landsat|Landsat]] — Landsat satellites capture images that are used for land cover and crop type mapping.
- [[tiles|Tiles]] — Tiles split large images into smaller, spatially contiguous pieces for parallel processing.
- [[masking|Masking]] — Masking removes unwanted pixels from images, such as those affected by clouds or shadows.
- [[geometric-correction|Geometric Correction]] — Raw images from satellites often have distortions; geometric correction removes these to produce a georeferenced image.
- [[cloud-masking|Cloud Masking]] — Cloud masking identifies and removes cloudy pixels in satellite images to ensure clear observations.
- [[quality-flags|Quality Flags]] — Satellite images often include quality bands that flag problematic pixels like clouds or shadows.
- [[orthophoto|Orthophoto]] — An orthophoto is a geometrically corrected image where distortions from terrain and camera have been removed.
- [[analysis-ready-data|Analysis Ready Data]] — Analysis Ready Data (ARD) are satellite images that have been processed to a consistent, analysis-ready state.
- [[satellite-image-time-series-2|Satellite image time series]] — By stacking images over time, we create a satellite image time series that captures change.
- [[satellite-image-classification|Satellite image classification]] — Satellite image classification assigns thematic labels to pixels or objects in satellite imagery.
- [[processing|Processing]] — Raw satellite images are refined through geometric and radiometric corrections during processing.
