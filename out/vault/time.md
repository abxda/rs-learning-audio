# Time / Tiempo

> **level 0** · *primitive* · sources: th_data_cubes

**What it is / Qué es.**

- **EN.** The [[temporal-dimension|temporal dimension]] in [[satellite-image-time-series|satellite image time series]], enabling [[detection|detection]] of change, [[phenology|phenology]], and trends through repeated observations.
- **ES.** [[temporal-dimension|Dimensión temporal]] en [[satellite-image-time-series|series temporales de imágenes satelitales]], que permite la [[detection|detección]] de cambios, [[phenology|fenología]] y tendencias mediante observaciones repetidas.

## This unlocks / Esto habilita
- [[time-series|Time series]] — A time series is a sequence of data points collected at successive, often equally spaced, times.
- [[monitoring|Monitoring]] — Monitoring involves systematic, repeated observation over time to detect changes or trends.
- [[season|Season]] — A season is a recurring period of time, such as a growing season for crops.
- [[data-cube|Data cube]] — The time dimension in a data-cube allows analysis of temporal changes across repeated satellite observations.
- [[growth|Growth]] — Growth is a temporal process observed over the growing season.
- [[phenology|Phenology]] — By observing vegetation over time, phenology tracks seasonal changes in plant development stages.
- [[spatiotemporal-array|Spatiotemporal array]] — A spatiotemporal array organizes satellite imagery along both spatial coordinates and a time dimension, enabling analysis of change.
- [[temporal-resolution|Temporal resolution]] — Temporal resolution describes how frequently a satellite revisits the same location, i.e., the time between successive images.
- [[near-real-time|Near real-time]] — Near real‑time processing delivers data or results within a short delay after acquisition, enabling timely monitoring.
- [[time-series-analysis|Time series analysis]] — Time series analysis examines sequential data over time to detect patterns and trends.
