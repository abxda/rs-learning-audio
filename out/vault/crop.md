# Crop / Cultivo

> **level 0** · *phenomenon* · sources: introduction

**What it is / Qué es.**

- **EN.** A cultivated plant grown for food, feed, or other uses, whose [[type|type]], [[planted-area|planted area]], and [[yield|yield]] are targets of [[agricultural-statistics|agricultural statistics]].
- **ES.** Planta cultivada para alimento, forraje u otros usos, cuyo [[type|tipo]], [[planted-area|área sembrada]] y [[yield|rendimiento]] son [[objectives|objetivos]] de las [[agricultural-statistics|estadísticas agrícolas]].

## This unlocks / Esto habilita
- [[agriculture|Agriculture]] — Agriculture is the practice of cultivating crops and raising livestock for food and fiber.
- [[yield|Yield]] — Yield is the amount of harvested crop product per unit area, typically for a specific crop.
- [[agricultural-statistics|Agricultural Statistics]] — Agricultural statistics quantify crop production, area, and yield using data from surveys and remote sensing.
- [[crop-type-mapping|Crop type mapping]] — Crop type mapping aims to delineate the spatial distribution of different crop types.
- [[season|Season]] — Crop development cycles are tied to seasons, affecting satellite image time series.
- [[crop-identification|Crop identification]] — Crop identification is the task of recognizing specific crop types from satellite imagery.
- [[crop-assessment|Crop Assessment]] — Crop assessment evaluates the condition, area, and yield of cultivated plants using multiple data sources.
- [[maize|Maize]] — Maize is a specific type of crop, a cereal grain widely cultivated for food and feed.
- [[growth|Growth]] — Growth describes how a crop increases in biomass and progresses through phenological stages.
- [[crop-types|Crop types]] — Crop types are the specific kinds of cultivated plants, such as maize or wheat, that define the output of agricultural mapping.
- [[planted-area|Planted area]] — Planted area is the total land extent in hectares where a specific crop has been deliberately sown or planted.
- [[phenology|Phenology]] — Phenology studies the timing of recurring biological events in crop life cycles, such as flowering.
- [[fallow|Fallow]] — Fallow land is temporarily without a crop, but remains part of agricultural land use.
- [[sowing|Sowing]] — Sowing is the agricultural practice of planting crop seeds into prepared soil to initiate a growing season.
- [[phenological-stage|Phenological stage]] — Crop development progresses through defined phenological stages from sowing to harvest.
- [[crop-yield|Crop yield]] — Crop yield is the yield of a cultivated plant, measured per unit area for agricultural statistics.
- [[crop-classification-models|Crop classification models]] — These models are trained to identify specific crop types from satellite imagery.
- [[large-scale-agricultural-monitoring|Large scale agricultural monitoring]] — It focuses on tracking crop conditions and extent across large agricultural areas.
- [[crop-mapping|Crop mapping]] — Crop mapping applies mapping techniques to identify and delineate cultivated plant species, using crop knowledge.
